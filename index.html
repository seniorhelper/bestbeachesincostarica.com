There is no single "best" beach. There's a best beach for you.

Ask ten travelers for the best beach in Costa Rica and you'll get ten different answers — and they're all kind of right. That's because this small country packs in an absurd amount of coastline variety. Within a few hours of driving you can go from glassy turquoise swimming bays to world-class surf breaks to wild jungle shores where monkeys watch you from the trees.

So instead of pretending one beach wins, this guide does something more useful. It sorts the coast by what you actually care about — calm water for the kids, a reliable wave, wildlife you'll remember forever, or a sunset and a cold drink with nobody around. Match the beach to the moment, and you'll never have a bad beach day here.

Below you'll find the four great coastal regions, deep profiles of the beaches worth crossing the country for, a straight answer on when to go, and the wildlife events that turn a beach trip into a story you tell for years. Let's find your sand.

The Lay Of The Land

Four coasts, four completely different moods

Costa Rica's beaches fall into four broad regions. Pick the region first — the perfect beach is almost always inside it.

01

Guanacaste

The Gold Coast · Pacific NW

The driest, sunniest corner of the country and the home of the big resorts. Golden sand, reliable blue skies, and the easiest access of all — fly straight into Liberia (LIR). If you want a sure thing in the dry season, you start here.

ConchalFlamingoTamarindoHermosaNacascolo
02

Nicoya Peninsula

Surf · Yoga · Blue Zone

Wilder, more rugged, and gloriously laid-back. This is surf-and-yoga country — Santa Teresa and Nosara's Playa Guiones lead the way — and it's also one of the world's five Blue Zones, where people famously live longer. Pure pura vida.

Santa TeresaGuionesSámaraMontezuma
03

Central & South Pacific

Jungle Meets Sea

Where the rainforest spills right onto the sand. Manuel Antonio puts monkeys and sloths minutes from golden beaches, while further south Uvita guards the famous Whale's Tail. The most "only-in-Costa-Rica" stretch of coast.

Manuel AntonioUvitaDominicalPavones
04

The Caribbean

Calm Water · Afro-Caribbean Soul

A different country, almost. Reggae drifts over palm-shaded coves, the water is the calmest and clearest in Costa Rica, and the crowds thin out. Punta Uva and Cahuita for swimming; remote Tortuguero for nesting sea turtles.

Punta UvaCoclesCahuitaTortuguero

The Heavy Hitters

Beaches worth crossing the country for

Five beaches that genuinely live up to the hype — and one wildcard the brochures forget.

01
Guanacaste · Gold Coast

Playa Conchal

Costa Rica's most photogenic swimming beach, and it has a secret: the "sand" is made of millions of crushed seashells. They don't cloud the water, so you get clarity up to 40 feet — the clearest on the Pacific. Snorkel the rocky ends, then watch the shells shift color as the sun moves. It earns every bit of its reputation.

02
South Pacific · Marino Ballena NP

Playa Uvita & the Whale's Tail

One of nature's strangest coincidences. At low tide, a sandbar emerges off Uvita shaped exactly like a humpback's tail — and humpback whales really do migrate through these waters to give birth. You can walk hundreds of meters out along the "tail," waves breaking on both sides. Time it with the tide chart; at high tide the magic vanishes underwater.

The beach that does it all. Golden sand framed by rainforest and the dramatic Cathedral Point, with a national park right behind it where monkeys, sloths and iguanas wander into view. Calm enough for families, beautiful enough for everyone, and reachable from San José. If you only have time for one Pacific beach, this is the safe, spectacular choice.

Santa Teresa

A dirt-road surf town that became a global icon without losing its soul. Reliable waves break all day, sunset is a community event, and the food scene punches absurdly above its weight. Timing Is Everything

When to go (and the trick most people miss)

The Pacific runs on two seasons. The dry season (December–April) is the sure bet: near-constant sunshine, peak prices, peak crowds. The green season (May–November) brings lush landscapes, lower prices, and reliable afternoon showers — mornings are often gorgeous.

Here's the trick: the two coasts don't always rain at the same time. When the Pacific is soggy, the southern Caribbean often has its driest, sunniest windows around September and October. Chase the sun across the country and you can beat the crowds and the rain at once.

Patterns shift year to year — always check a current forecast before you book.

The beach moments you'll never forget

In Costa Rica the wildlife comes to the beach. Time your trip around these and you'll go home with the best story at the dinner table.

Humpback Whales

Marino Ballena off Uvita hosts one of the longest whale-watching seasons on Earth, as humpbacks arrive from both hemispheres to breed and birth in the warm bay.

Aug–Oct & Dec–Apr

Turtle Arribadas

At Playa Ostional, thousands of olive ridley turtles storm ashore at once to nest — one of the planet's great wildlife spectacles. Tortuguero hosts green turtles on the Caribbean.

Ostional Aug–Oct · Tortuguero Jun–Oct

Monkeys & Sloths

At Manuel Antonio and Cahuita, the rainforest meets the sand. White-faced capuchins raid the picnic tables and sloths doze overhead while you swim below.

Year-round

World-Class Surf

From beginner whitewash at Tamarindo to Pavones — home to one of the longest left-hand waves on Earth, where a single ride can last over a minute.

Year-round · biggest swells May–Nov
